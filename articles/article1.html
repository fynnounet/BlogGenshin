Understanding the Pity System

Everything new travelers need to know about pity, soft pity, hard pity, and guaranteed pulls — before spending a single Primogem.

★ Beginner-Friendly · 6 min read · Last updated: Ver 5.x

What is Pity?

Every time you pull (wish) on a banner without getting a 5-star, your pity counter goes up by 1. The system guarantees that you cannot pull forever without receiving a 5-star — eventually, the game forces one to appear. This is pity. There are actually two pity thresholds you need to know: soft pity and hard pity.

Pull 1 Soft pity starts at ~74 Hard pity at 90

Soft Pity vs Hard Pity

Soft pity kicks in around pull 74 on Character banners. From this point, your odds of hitting a 5-star increase dramatically with each successive pull — instead of the base ~0.6% rate, it climbs sharply until it reaches 100% at hard pity.

Hard pity is the absolute maximum number of pulls before a guaranteed 5-star. You will never exceed this number without receiving one.

The 50/50 System

On limited Character Event banners, when you receive a 5-star, there is a 50% chance it will be the featured limited character and a 50% chance it will be a standard character (Diluc, Jean, Mona, Qiqi, Keqing, Tighnari, Yumemizuki Mizuki or Lohen ).

★ Guaranteed Pull
If you lose the 50/50 (receive a standard character), you are guaranteed to win the next 50/50. This is sometimes called being "on guarantee." Your guaranteed status carries over to the next banner of the same type — even after the banner ends.
